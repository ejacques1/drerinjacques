Week 2 — Build

Start these today. They run in the background all week.

Three things to kick off now, because they take a few days to clear. Do them first, then we build.

Do this today #1

Buy your domain.

Get it now so it's ready to connect next week. You won't hook it up yet, but owning it early means no waiting later.

  • Where: a real domain registrar. I use GoDaddy, and there are others (Namecheap, Cloudflare, Google/Squarespace Domains).
⚠️ Heads up: a newly registered domain is locked for about 60 days before it can move to another provider. That's normal. The thing to avoid is buying your domain inside an all-in-one website builder that makes it hard to point it wherever you want. Buy it from a dedicated registrar so you fully own it and can connect it to anything.
Do this today #2

Start Stripe, your business, and your EIN.

You won't use Stripe until Week 3, but the setup takes days, so start it now and let it process while you build.

  • Get your EIN. You need it to open a merchant account on Stripe.
  • Start your Stripe account and begin the merchant setup.
  • Start your business entity so everything is in your business's name.

These are the payment rails. Week 3 is where you connect them. Starting today means they're ready when you get there.

Do this today #3

If your LLC is slow, use a DBA.

Some states take weeks to form an LLC. A DBA can get you running in a day or two, and it's enough to move forward.

  • A DBA lets you get an EIN.
  • A DBA lets you open a merchant account on Stripe.

This is what I did, it's not legal advice. I used LegalZoom, again, just what I did, not an endorsement. See the entity-types resource for your options.
