Brand it

Make it look like a real company.

Your logo. You don't need a designer. You ask Claude, and it makes you look established instead of generic.

Favicon

The tiny icon in your browser tab.

Where you've seen it: the little icon next to each site's name up in your tabs.

Examples: Google's colored G · YouTube's play button · Amazon's smile.

Why it matters: yours stands out in a sea of tabs. No favicon = a plain gray globe = looks unfinished.

Social preview image (OG image)

The picture that pops up when you paste your link.

Where you've seen it: drop a link in a text or on Instagram and a picture with a headline appears instead of a plain link.

Why it matters: a rich preview gets far more clicks and looks trustworthy. A bare link looks broken or like spam.

Claude makes all three at once.

Make it legit

Why this matters: you're about to take payments and collect people's info. These pages protect you, and payment processors like Stripe expect them. This is what separates a real business from a hobby.

Step 1 — Create them.

Paste into Claude
Create the legal pages my platform needs: a terms and conditions page, a privacy policy, a cookies notice, and a refund policy. Base them on a standard subscription web app, link them in my footer, then commit and push.
⚠️ Not legal advice. This is a strong starting point. If you're in something high-risk or regulated, have a professional review it.

Step 2 — Compare. Find a platform like yours, open their terms, and have Claude check yours against it.

Paste into Claude
Here is a similar platform's terms page: [paste the link or the text]. Compare it to mine and tell me what I'm missing, then update my pages.

Step 3 — Add it to signup. The part people forget: it's not enough to have the pages, people have to agree to them.

Paste into Claude
Add a checkbox to my sign-up form that says "I agree to the Terms and Conditions and Privacy Policy," with links to those pages. Don't let anyone sign up unless it's checked. Commit and push.
