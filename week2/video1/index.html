Step 1

Find proven ideas + your competitors.

Don't guess. Look at what's already selling. There are marketplaces where founders list their products with real revenue attached, because to buy or sell a business, the seller has to show their numbers.

That makes them a window into what's actually making money, not just what sounds good.

How to use them: filter by revenue, study what sells and why, then reverse-engineer it and make it better. You're not copying. You're proving people already pay for this.

Step 2

Capture the designs you love.

Once you find platforms whose format you like, capture the design so Claude can build from it. The tool for this is SkillUI. It's free, no API key, and it's a simple command you run.

  • Point it at a site with one commandIt pulls that site's colors, fonts, spacing, and layout into design files, plus screenshots.
  • Those files are plain markdownA simple version of the design. Nothing technical. It's the format Claude reads and builds from best.
  • Open the folder with ClaudeClaude reads them automatically and builds in that style. You're not describing your design from scratch. You're showing it.
skillui --url yoursite.com --mode ultra

Lock that in. It shapes everything you build next.

Quick note

What about API keys?

  • No key needed: content or pre-made quizzesExample: a course with lessons and a fixed quiz. The questions and answers are already written in, so nothing has to call AI live.
  • Key needed: a live AI featureExample: a tool where someone uploads their essay or document and your platform reads it and gives feedback on the spot. That calls AI live every time, so it needs a key and a small cost per use.

Most of you won't need one to start.
