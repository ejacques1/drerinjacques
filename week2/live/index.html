The big idea for Week 2

Build from information, not from your head.

Most people start with a solution they dreamed up and go build it. You're going to start with a problem someone will pay to solve, then build.

Don't build "a platform." Solve one problem, better than anyone, for one specific person.

Sometimes the hard part is naming that problem. So let me show you how I think about it, using my own product.

My example first

Chatify: who am I really up against?

I'm in the link-in-bio world. But look, each player there solves a different problem:

Linktree — "I can only put one link in my bio, but I have a lot to share."
Stan Store — "I want to sell my digital products straight from my bio, no website."
Chatify (me) — "People visit my link and leave, and I never capture who they are."
My angle: Linktree gives you a link. Stan lets you sell. I capture the person, so your traffic turns into leads you can actually follow up with. That's the one problem I solve.
One you already know

Even Netflix has competitors.

They're all "streaming," but not one of them solves the exact same problem:

Netflix — "I want to watch what I want, when I want, huge library and originals."
Hulu — "I want to keep up with shows airing on TV right now."
Disney+ — "I want family-safe content and all the franchises in one place."
Max — "I want the premium, prestige shows and big movies."
Each one picked a specific problem for a specific person. That's your job too. Find your set, then be sharper than all of them for one person.
Now you

Find your own set.

Who are the main players you're watching? What problem does each of them solve? And what's the one you'll solve better? You're not inventing from thin air, you're finding proven demand and getting sharper.

The videos show you exactly where to look: the marketplaces (TrustMRR and others) where you can see which problems are actually making money.

New tool this week

SkillUI.

Think of it like a GitHub tool you add in as a skill. Once it's in, you point it at any website you love, and it makes a markdown file, a plain-text version of that site's design.

  • Markdown is just the format AI reads best
  • It uses way fewer tokens than screenshots, so it's cheaper and cleaner for Claude to build from
  • You end up building your look from the best parts of a few sites, not copying any one
