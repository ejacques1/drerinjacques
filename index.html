The Authority Signals Framework™

Understanding which sources AI systems prioritize requires asking four critical questions

Institutional Affiliation

"Who published it?"

Institutional Affiliation answers "Who published it?" by categorizing sources by organization type (.edu, .gov, .com) to reveal how institutional credibility influences health citations.

Author Credentials

"Who wrote it?"

Author Credentials answers "Who wrote it?" by capturing individual-level expertise through professional degrees, certifications, and medical specialization. Variables range from no author attribution to visible medical credentials with specialty identification.

Quality Assurance

"How was it vetted?"

Quality Assurance answers "How was it vetted?" by documenting editorial processes and evidence standards through presence of medical review statements, content recency, and citation of supporting references.

Digital Authority

"How does AI find it?"

Digital Authority answers "How does AI find it?" by quantifying web-based credibility signals including Moz Page Authority, Domain Authority, spam scores, schema markup implementation, and content length.

The Research

Systematically documenting how AI platforms evaluate and cite sources across the ecosystem. Each study captures baseline patterns before optimization becomes widespread.
